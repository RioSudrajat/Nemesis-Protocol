## TL;DR

NEMESIS is a DePIN protocol on Solana designed to finance, verify, and distribute cashflows from **productive EV infrastructure assets**.

The long-term vision is broad:

- productive mobility assets
- charging infrastructure
- energy infrastructure

But the phase 1 launch wedge is deliberately narrow and realistic:

> **productive mobility assets in Indonesia, starting with ride-hailing EV rental bikes and other income-producing EV fleet vehicles**

In the first module, investor capital funds productive EV assets. GPS-based telemetry verifies daily route logs, distance traveled, active usage hours, movement consistency, and maintenance status. The assets generate real recurring payments through rental, rent-to-own, or contracted remittance models. Those cashflows are then split into:

- investor yield
- principal recovery
- maintenance reserve
- default reserve
- operator fees
- protocol fees

This is not a speculative token story.

It is a cashflow protocol for real EV infrastructure.

## How the First Product Works

The first Nemesis product is not a loose tokenized vehicle share.

It is a:

> **36-month mobility credit pool / rent-to-own pool**

This product is designed for assets like ride-hailing EV rental bikes where the asset generates fixed recurring cashflow.

### Base Planning Assumptions Per Unit

- EV bike + telemetry + setup + onboarding = **Rp 25,000,000**
- monthly inflow per productive unit = **Rp 1,500,000**

That inflow is then split using the phase 1 agreed structure:

- **45% Principal Recovery**
- **20% Investor Yield**
- **10% Maintenance Reserve**
- **5% Default Reserve**
- **8% Operator Base Fee**
- **2% Operator Performance Fee**
- **10% Protocol Fee**

This creates:

- real investor yield
- gradual principal recovery
- operator incentives
- reserve-backed safety buffers
- real protocol revenue

---

## Why This Model Is Stronger Than Pure Revenue Splits

A pure top-line revenue split is too fragile for phase 1.

It over-relies on:

- self-reported revenue
- unstable margins
- inconsistent operator economics

The rent-to-own / mobility credit pool structure is better because:

- principal recovery is explicit
- yield is explicit
- operator incentives are explicit
- reserve logic is explicit
- maturity settlement is easier to model

This is much stronger for productive EV bikes and similar mobility assets.

## Why Default Reserve Matters

Default reserve exists to protect the pool from real-world disruptions such as:

- late payment
- temporary driver default
- collection delays
- reassignment downtime
- short operational interruptions

It acts as a shock absorber so the pool can remain stable while the operator or protocol fixes the issue.

Without it, even a small number of underperforming units can destabilize investor distributions.

---

## Why Maintenance Reserve Matters

Maintenance reserve exists to keep the asset alive:

- routine service
- brake and tire work
- small part replacement
- scheduled maintenance
- basic operational health

This is a critical component of DePIN credibility. Productive assets must remain productive.

---

## Why Operator Economics Must Be Explicit

The operator is a core actor in the system.

For mobility assets, the operator is often responsible for:

- onboarding the driver
- collections
- reassignment
- field operations
- servicing coordination
- day-to-day performance enforcement

That is why Nemesis gives the operator:

- **8% base fee**
- **2% performance fee**

This keeps the operator aligned without letting pool economics become vague.
