## Hook

Indonesia processes over **100 million e-commerce packages per day**. It has **4.3 million ride-hailing drivers**. Its food delivery market generates billions in annual GMV. Every single one of these transactions depends on a physical vehicle making a physical trip.

EV adoption is accelerating. The government targets **13 million EVs on the road by 2030**. Fleet operators like Electrum — backed by a **$1 billion joint venture between GoTo and TBS Energi** — are deploying electric motorcycles across the country. This is one of the largest physical infrastructure buildouts in Southeast Asia.

Yet today, there is **no way for everyday Indonesians to co-invest in this productive fleet and earn from its daily operations**. There is no transparent, verifiable record of how these vehicles perform. Fleet operators depend on bank debt at 12–15% interest. Investors are locked out entirely.

**What if every kilometer driven — by an ojol, a courier, a delivery driver — generated verifiable, on-chain yield, distributed directly to investors' wallets every single week?**

That is NEMESIS.

## The Problem

### 1. The EV Financing Gap
Productive EV vehicles — whether electric motorcycles for ride-hailing or electric cargo bikes for logistics — cost Rp 20–50 million per unit. Most operators cannot purchase them outright. Fleet operators must finance acquisition through banks at **12–15% annual interest**, creating a structural capital constraint that slows EV adoption precisely when demand is highest. The bottleneck is not market demand. It is capital access.

### 2. The Investment Gap
Indonesia's 17 million crypto users have nowhere to invest in productive physical assets with transparent, yield-generating returns:

| Investment Type | APY | Risk Profile |
|---|---|---|
| Bank deposit (deposito) | 5–7% | Capital guaranteed |
| Government bonds (SBN) | 6–7% | Capital guaranteed |
| P2P lending | 12–18% | High — default risk, no collateral |
| Crypto speculation | Highly variable | Very High |
| **Productive EV investment (NEMESIS)** | **~30–41%** | **Asset-backed, GPS-verified, on-chain** |

There is no accessible, asset-backed investment product tied to productive physical infrastructure in Indonesia. Bank deposits are too low. P2P lending carries serious default risk with no underlying asset. NEMESIS fills this gap with a new asset class: revenue-generating EV vehicles on Solana.

### 3. Zero Transparency in Fleet Operations
Today, fleet operators self-report revenue. Investors must accept operator claims with no independent verification mechanism. There is no on-chain record of vehicle activity, no proof of maintenance, and no accountability layer if operators misreport. Investors are exposed to information asymmetry with no remedy.

### 4. The Southeast Asia DePIN Gap
DePIN (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Networks) is a $19B+ sector globally. Yet **zero DePIN mobility protocols operate in Southeast Asia today**. MVL/TADA tokenizes a ride-hailing platform but provides no direct yield — its token was delisted from Bitget in July 2025. DeCharge focuses on EV charging infrastructure, not mobility operations. DIMO monetizes vehicle data but requires a $300 OBD hardware device and is US-centric. The entire region of 680 million people has no operational DePIN protocol for productive EV vehicles. NEMESIS is the first.

### Step 4 — Proof of Activity Layer *(GPS-Based — Key Differentiator)*

This is what makes NEMESIS fundamentally different from every other fleet investment product in existence. Every vehicle on the network is a **live data node**, continuously generating verifiable proof of its operations on-chain. No competitor in the mobility or DePIN space offers this for the Indonesian productive EV context.

---

#### 4.1 — Trip Tracking

The GPS tracker installed on each vehicle streams telemetry continuously:

- **Frequency:** Position update every 10 seconds
- **Data captured per trip:** Start coordinates, end coordinates, total distance (km), duration (minutes), average speed, timestamp, vehicle category (ojol / courier / logistics)
- **Trip detection logic:** Vehicle departs from base zone + sustained movement >5 minutes + speed >10 km/h
- **Daily summary compiled:** Total trips, total km driven, total active hours, idle time percentage
- **On-chain submission:** Daily activity summary is hashed and submitted to Solana at end of day → creates an immutable, explorer-verifiable Proof of Activity

> Anyone — investor, regulator, or auditor — can query the Solana chain and verify that vehicle #NMS-0042 drove 57.3 km across 18 delivery trips on any given day. This is not a report. It is cryptographic proof.

---

#### 4.2 — Activity Validation (Gatekeeper)

The Proof of Activity layer acts as a **gatekeeper** for the revenue smart contract. A driver cannot submit revenue without first passing the GPS activity check:

```
Operator submits: "Vehicle earned Rp 200,000 today"
         │
         ▼
Smart Contract checks GPS hash for today:
  ✓ Vehicle moved ≥20 km?
  ✓ Active ≥3 hours?
  ✓ Trip pattern consistent with reported category?
         │
   ┌─────┴─────┐
   ✓ PASS       ✗ FAIL
   │             │
Revenue          Submission
accepted         rejected /
                 flagged for
                 operator review
```

**What this prevents:**
- Fabricated revenue reports from idle vehicles
- Inflated earnings claims across any vehicle category
- Ghost vehicle fraud (vehicle parked, fake revenue submitted)

This validation mechanism works identically whether the vehicle is an ojol, a delivery motorcycle, or a logistics cargo bike — the GPS-based activity check is agnostic to revenue category.

#### 4.3 — Driver / Operator Node Score

Every vehicle operator on NEMESIS has an **on-chain Node Score** — a reputation metric that reflects their operational quality and reliability. This creates accountability without manual oversight.

**Node Score (0–100) is calculated from:**

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Consistency Rate | 30% | Active days ÷ total enrolled days |
| Daily Distance | 25% | Average km/day vs fleet benchmark for that vehicle category |
| Operating Hours | 25% | Average active hours/day |
| Revenue Correlation | 20% | Reported revenue vs GPS-inferred activity match |

**Score outcomes:**
- **Score 80–100:** Priority for new vehicle assignments, reduced security deposit, eligible for premium fleet tiers
- **Score 50–79:** Standard status
- **Score below 50:** Flagged for operator review, contract eligibility at risk

Node Score is **fully on-chain** — visible to fleet operators, investors, and the driver themselves. Operators have strong incentive to maintain high scores because it directly affects vehicle access and contract terms.

#### 4.4 — Maintenance Proof (Mileage-Based)

The GPS tracker accumulates a **lifetime odometer** — a running total of kilometers since vehicle deployment. NEMESIS smart contracts use this to enforce an automated, verifiable maintenance schedule:

```
MAINTENANCE TRIGGER SCHEDULE (configurable per vehicle type):

  2,500 km  → Basic inspection (tires, lights, brakes check)
  5,000 km  → Brake system + battery health inspection
 10,000 km  → Full service (all mechanical systems)
 20,000 km  → Major overhaul / battery capacity assessment

When threshold reached:
  → Alert sent to driver wallet + operator dashboard
  → 7-day grace window to complete service
  → Driver submits: service receipt photo hash +
    authorized technician wallet signature
  → Maintenance Fund (3% accumulated daily) releases
    IDRX to pay for verified service
```

**If maintenance is not completed within 7 days:**
- Vehicle flagged `MAINTENANCE_OVERDUE` on-chain
- Investor dashboards show warning badge
- Vehicle excluded from active pool count for yield calculation
- Operator is notified of escalation risk

Investors never have to wonder if the vehicle they co-own is being properly maintained. The maintenance fund is built into the protocol, funded automatically from daily revenue, and proof of service is cryptographically anchored on-chain. This level of asset transparency does not exist in any traditional fleet investment product anywhere in the world.
